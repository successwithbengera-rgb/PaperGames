What is this?

RS-PK — the Ruby & Sapphire through Power Keepers stretch of the Pokémon TCG — is one of the most skill-expressive, game-variety-rich formats the game has ever had. It's a format I love deeply. But the cards? They're old. Low resolution, washed out scans, cards that look rough on a modern printer.

So I fixed it. Every single card in this Gardevoir deck has been upscaled and sharpened to crisp, print-ready quality. The main monsters — Gardevoir, Gardevoir ex — went further: full redesigns with new commissioned-style art, dimensional out-of-border composition. And I built a custom Cell token from scratch to represent the deck's core engine.

Gardevoir's whole game is built around Imprison — locking your opponent's Pokémon into a cell, controlling what they can and can't do. The Cell token makes that mechanic physical and real on the table.

What was redesigned?

Not every card needed new art — most of the set just needed to be cleaned up and made sharp. But the headline monsters deserved more. Gardevoir and Gardevoir ex both got fully reimagined illustrations: dimensional, out-of-border compositions that break the card frame the way modern Pokémon TCG does with its premium alts.

The rest of the 11 redesigned cards got updated layouts — tighter typography, cleaner energy icons, modernized card borders — while staying faithful to the RS-PK aesthetic. This isn't a reskin. It's a restoration with upgrades.

Original era cards (top) vs. modernized versions (bottom) — same era, sharper execution.

The Cell Token

Gardevoir's main play pattern revolves around Imprison — attaching a marker to your opponent's Active Pokémon and restricting what it can do. In competitive play you track this with any random card or coin. That felt wrong for something this central to the deck's identity.

I designed a dedicated Cell token: a steel prison-gate medallion, dark and industrial, with "CELL" stamped at top and bottom. It reads immediately on the table. When you drop a Cell token on someone's Pokémon, both players know exactly what it means.

Cell token — custom-designed marker for Gardevoir's Imprison mechanic.

Why print this?

RS-PK is a format worth playing in 2025. It rewards real game knowledge — sequencing, resource management, matchup understanding — in ways that modern Pokémon TCG doesn't always demand. But the original cards are expensive to acquire in good condition, and low-quality scans ruin the table experience.

This project is my answer to that. A full proxy deck you can actually be proud to sleeve up. Sharp, polished, cohesive. The Gardevoir deck as it should look if it were released today.
